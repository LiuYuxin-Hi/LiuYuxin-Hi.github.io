This study investigates how perceived quality of home- and community-based services and urban-rural disparities influence aging-in-place intentions among older adults in China.

## Introduction {#introduction}

Aging-in-place is a major concern in China due to rapid urbanization and an increasing elderly population. This paper explores the role of home- and community-based services (HCBS) quality and urban-rural differences on older adults’ intentions to age in place.

## Methods {#methods}

- National survey of Chinese older adults
- Assessment of perceived HCBS quality
- Urban-rural comparisons
- Statistical modeling of aging-in-place intentions

## Results {#results}

- Urban residents reported higher satisfaction with HCBS
- Better perceived quality strongly associated with aging-in-place intentions
- Significant disparities exist between urban and rural populations

## Discussion {#discussion}

The study highlights service gaps that may impact the ability of older adults to age in place. Policymakers should focus on improving rural HCBS and reducing urban-rural disparities.

## Conclusion {#conclusion}

Quality of services and regional disparities significantly affect aging-in-place intentions. Targeted interventions can help promote equitable aging opportunities.
